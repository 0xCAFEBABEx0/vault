## Overview
Technical report for RhinoVLA, a Vision-Language-Action model designed for embodied AI and robotic manipulation tasks.

## Key Contributions
- End-to-end VLA architecture for robotic control
- Integration of vision, language understanding, and action generation
- Large-scale training on diverse robotic manipulation datasets

## Architecture
- Vision encoder for scene understanding
- Language encoder for instruction following
- Action decoder generating continuous control signals
- Cross-modal attention mechanisms linking vision, language, and action

## Training
- Pre-training on large video and robotics datasets
- Fine-tuning on specific manipulation tasks
- Data augmentation and domain randomization strategies

## Results
- Strong performance on standard robotic manipulation benchmarks
- Generalization to novel objects and environments
- Real-time inference suitable for closed-loop control

## Significance
Represents progress toward general-purpose robotic foundation models that can understand natural language instructions and perform complex manipulation tasks.
