## Overview
Proposes a re-imagined framework for ISO 26262 functional safety standards in the context of autonomous vehicles. The paper argues for enhancing controllability through improved transferability and predictability of safety requirements.

## Key Contributions
- Analysis of limitations of current ISO 26262 for autonomous driving
- Framework linking controllability to transferability and predictability
- Practical guidelines for safety case development

## Analysis
- Review of current controllability definitions and their inadequacy for SAE Level 4-5 autonomy
- Transferability: ensuring safety properties transfer across operational design domains
- Predictability: enabling reliable prediction of system behavior in novel scenarios

## Proposed Framework
- Extended controllability metrics incorporating transferability scores
- Predictability-based safety argumentation patterns
- Integration with existing automotive safety processes

## Significance
Addresses a critical gap in autonomous vehicle safety standards, providing a path toward more robust and adaptive functional safety frameworks.
